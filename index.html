01 — Problem & Customer

Hostel laundry is broken.

1200+ students. 8–12 machines. Zero visibility. Indian hostel operators lose thousands of student-hours annually to laundry confusion — and maintenance teams fly blind until machines physically fail.

PAIN 01

Students physically walk to laundry rooms to check machine availability. Wasted trips, peak-hour crowding, zero queue intelligence or slot prediction.

PAIN 02

Faults are reported only after machines break. No anomaly detection, no predictive alerts — downtime is prolonged, repair costs spike, and student satisfaction collapses.

Laundry rooms swing between ghost towns and war zones. Operators cannot plan staffing proactively, and students cannot plan their week — because nobody predicts peak demand.

02 — Solution & Product

Built for the problem. Priced for the market.

SmartWash retrofits any existing washer with a ₹6K–₹10K sensor kit and connects it to an AI backend that predicts demand, detects faults before breakdown, manages queues, and notifies students — all in real time, with no proprietary hardware required.

Tech Stack — With Justification
HW

ESP32 + ACS712 + SW-420

Why ESP32: Dual-core, WiFi+BT onboard, rich GPIO, ₹350/unit. No companion chip needed. FreeRTOS enables concurrent sensor sampling + MQTT publishing.
Why ACS712: Non-invasive current sensing without modifying washer wiring. Detects machine ON/OFF state from power draw.
Why SW-420: Vibration presence confirms machine is in active spin/wash cycle — eliminates false idle readings from standby power draw.

API

Flask REST — 10 Endpoints

Why Flask: Lightweight, Python-native, pairs cleanly with scikit-learn and Prophet with no ORM overhead. 10 endpoints cover machine state, booking, queue, fault, analytics, FCM push. firebase-admin SDK manages DB writes. python-dotenv keeps secrets out of code.

DB

Firebase Realtime Database

Why Firebase: Sub-100ms sync to all connected PWA clients without polling. Free tier covers 50+ machines. Offline persistence means ESP32 writes survive brief WiFi drops. Schema is hierarchical per machine — clean, predictable, debuggable.

AI

Isolation Forest + Facebook Prophet

Why Isolation Forest: Unsupervised, works with small datasets, no labelled fault examples required. Per-machine model trained on its unique current-vibration baseline — generalises across washer brands.
Why Prophet: Handles seasonality (weekly laundry peaks), holiday effects, and missing data natively. 48-hour forecasts written to Firebase every 6 hours.

UI

Mobile-First PWA

Why PWA: Zero app-store friction. Works on ₹8K Android phones with weak WiFi. Service worker caches last known state — machine grid still loads if backend is slow. FCM push notifications work natively through the browser.

10 — Why SmartWash Wins

The moat gets deeper over time.

Haier can cut prices. They cannot replicate a data flywheel trained on years of real wash patterns from hundreds of Indian campuses. Every machine deployed widens our lead.

Data Flywheel Moat

Every deployed machine generates training data. More machines → better anomaly models → fewer false alarms → higher NPS → more campuses. Haier's closed ecosystem cannot build this flywheel in the Indian hostel segment — their data lives inside proprietary hardware that brands won't share.

Hardware Agnosticism as a Feature

Institutions already own washers. We don't ask them to replace ₹25K–₹40K machines. We ask them to spend ₹8K to make those machines smart. This is the only pitch that works with a hostel warden's actual budget — and it makes every existing washer a potential customer.

Institutional Stickiness

Once 500 students use booking, queue, and push notifications daily, the switching cost is social — not just technical. Students won't accept going back to walking to check machine availability. Churn becomes a political problem for the warden, not a product problem for us.

Global Flexibility via Open Stack

Swap Firebase for local servers in China. Use LoRa in African campuses with poor WiFi. Integrate UPI/PayTM, M-Pesa, or GrabPay per market. No proprietary protocol blocks adaptation. Every configuration change is a new market, not a new product.
